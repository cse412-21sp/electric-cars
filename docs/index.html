Abstract

Global climate is changing due to the increased anthropogenic emissions of greenhouse gas. Although climate is changing on a global scale, it is very important to examine trends on a local scale, in order to best serve communities. Currently, in Washington State, we are in a transition period, with legislators making strides toward passing more aggressive climate policy, including phasing out the use of gasoline- and diesel-powered vehicles, and implementing a cap-and-trade industrial carbon program.

In this project, we explore changes in Washington State climatology over time, specifically looking at variables such as precipitation and annual heating and cooling days. Next, we take a look at air quality over time. Air quality can be influenced by climate variables, specifically temperature and precipitation and number of wildfires, but it is also highly dependent on the use of cars and other pollutant emitters. Finally, we look at how Washington state residents are combating climate change through the use of electric cars (as opposed to traditional gasoline and diesel vehicles).

Annual Cooling & Heating

Next, we are going to look into the annual cooling and heating days in Washington state to understand the climatology further. Just want to give a brief introduction to those who might not be familiar with annual cooling and heating days. Cooling degree days, abbreviated as CDD, and heating degree days, abbreviated as HDD are a standard metric calculated using a day’s average temperature. For example, if the average temperature is 65 F or below, the CDD is 0 (no need for cooling). If the temperature is above 65 F, the difference between 65 and the actual temperature is the CDD value. For example, a day with the temperature of 80 F would have a value of 15 CDD. The same concept, but opposite applies for HDD.

In this section, we created the two types of visualizations, average and sum for both CDD and HDD to see the differences between average and sum. Move mouse over to see detailed information and highlighted parts in each county or Search by county names.


Based on the above visualization, we notice that the highest in average CDD and HDD do not mean the highest sum of CDD and HDD. For the sum of CDD and HDD, King county has the highest in all the counties.

When comparing the future projection of HDD and CDD, the numbers are both positive, this means we will need more cooling and more heating in the future, basically more climate extremes.

Gasoline Component Emission

The air quality can be influenced by many factors. As PM 2.5 is one of the main pollutants, we also considered that gasoline emission from vehicles would take a great part as the pollutant to the air quality. Carbon dioxide (CO2) is the majority pollutant emitted from motor vehicles that contribute to global climate change.


This dot plot chart above gives a quick view on carbon dioxide emission in Washington State over the past 28 years from 1990 to 2018. The emission of carbon dioxide reached a peak at about 79.31 million metric tons in 2007. The orange colored trend line in the chart shows an increasing tendency during the past 28 years.

The carbon dioxide emissions fluctuate greatly every year in Washington State, but the general trend is still rising. Although gasoline emission is one of the important sources of CO2 emission but not the main source, it is still necessary to reduce the gasoline emission both for the environment and our health. Therefore, electric cars are a good way to reduce gasoline emission.

Electric Car

Amount of Electric Car Transactions (2010-2021)


From 2010 to 2020, the annual amount of electric car transactions in Washington state increased drastically from 109 to 89,028.


Amount of Electric Car Transactions by County (2010-2020)


Let’s take a closer look at the individual counties’ transactions throughout each month. Most of the transactions took place in King county(pink line), which made sense when considering the population of the county.

Beginning August 2019, Washington state offered tax exemptions for selected clean vehicles, which means we should expect an increase in the number of transactions, and we do. However, the pandemic affected people’s spending habits, which might have caused the fluctuations in transactions in 2020.


Primary Usage of Electric Cars


Looking at the primary purposes of electric cars, most people use it for ‘passenger’ purposes which is defined as transporting passengers. The next most popular use was ‘exempt’. According to the Washington State Legislature, ‘exempt’ refers to vehicles owned by a city, county, state government agency, or Indian tribe located in Washington state.
